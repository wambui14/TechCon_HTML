About TechCon 2024

History: The Origins and Evolution of TechCon

TechCon was founded in 2016 by a small group of passionate developers, educators, and tech entrepreneurs who shared a vision — to create a platform where innovation, collaboration, and learning could thrive. What started as a local meetup in Nairobi quickly gained momentum, drawing in a diverse community of technologists eager to share ideas and push boundaries. As the tech world evolved, so did TechCon. From its humble beginnings, the event grew into a major annual conference, attracting thought leaders, industry pioneers, and thousands of attendees from around the world. Over the years, TechCon has showcased everything from the rise of mobile development and cloud computing to the latest breakthroughs in artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and immersive technologies. Now in its 8th year, TechCon stands as a global symbol of innovation, growth, and community. Each conference builds on the legacy of the last, continuing to inspire a new generation of thinkers, builders, and change-makers who are shaping the future of technology.

Mission: Our Goals and Driving Principles

At TechCon, our mission is to ignite innovation, empower collaboration, and shape the future of technology through meaningful connections and knowledge-sharing. We believe that when diverse minds come together, extraordinary breakthroughs happen.
Our core goals include:

  • Providing an inclusive platform for tech professionals, students, and enthusiasts to learn, grow, and lead.
  • Showcasing emerging technologies that have the potential to transform industries and societies.
  • Supporting underrepresented voices in tech to ensure a more equitable and forward-thinking community.
  • Encouraging hands-on learning through workshops, demos, and real-world projects.
Guided by curiosity, creativity, and community, TechCon is committed to being more than just an event — we’re a movement toward a smarter, more connected world.

Past Speakers: Content Ideas

Speaker 1: Dr. Amina Mwangi

Bio: A respected AI researcher and professor at Nairobi Tech University, Dr. Mwangi is known for her groundbreaking work in natural language processing and machine learning in African languages. At TechCon 2021, she delivered a powerful keynote on ethical AI and the importance of representation in training data.

Speaker 2: James Okello

Bio: Co-founder of DevLoop Africa, James is a full-stack engineer and community builder who has led major projects in fintech across East Africa. In 2019, he ran a hands-on workshop at TechCon on scalable app development using JavaScript and Node.js.

Speaker 3: Lilian Wanjiru

Bio: A cybersecurity specialist and global consultant, Lilian has worked with organizations like Mozilla and Google. Her 2022 session focused on data privacy challenges in African tech startups, leaving the audience empowered and inspired to prioritize security by design.
